Text Analytics - Classification and Clustering

Description

Text classification and clustering form an integral part of analysing text data. In most business scenarios, it is important to analyse the text as a whole and as part of certain subgroups, which might not be apparent from simple frequency analysis. This is where clustering becomes necessary. Text Clustering helps one identify the underlying themes in text data, while also suggesting the number of themes that might be present in the data.

Text Classification, on the other hand, deals with predicting the category of text data. While sentiment analysis, is one kind of text analytics, it might not be enough in scenarios where the data belongs to more than 2-3 categories, or in scenarios where the text needs to be grouped based on the product/company the text talks about.

Learning Objective

This course is designed to equip a learner with all the necessary concepts and skills required to cluster and classify text data, while also validating if the model is a good fit using training-validation sets.

The course is divided into two modules.

First module will cover the concepts and techniques required for 'clustering' text data. The learner will acquire hands on experiences through a real business case prepared with real data. This module also has a downloadable reading content and four self assessment quizzes.

Second module has five simulations (real business cases with real data), six quizzes, and one real business case exercise with real data for self assessment. It covers the concepts of ‘Text Classification' using various algorithms such as Random Forest, Decision Trees, SVM and SLDA. The module also teaches about cross validating the classification model and the techniques used to reduce overfitting.
